# Slide 1: What Is The EEAI Hackathon?

- The Electrical Engineering and AI Hackathon challenges students to apply AI tools to real engineering problems involving diagrams, systems, and technical reasoning.
- Teams will explore how AI agents, prompting, and engineering workflows can help turn complex technical information into useful outputs.
- The event is designed to be practical, collaborative, and public-facing, with a focus on building credible solutions rather than just ideas.

# Slide 2: How Participants Will Work

- Participants will receive workshops on AI agents and agentic processes, with guidance on how to use expert prompting to produce strong work.
- A baseline solution will be provided so teams can start quickly, learn from an initial approach, and improve or extend it.
- Students can attend workshops during the week, receive expert mentoring, and take part in the coding allnighter while working with their team.

# Slide 3: Why Students Should Take Part

- Entries will be judged by expert Electrical Engineers, giving students exposure to professional expectations and technical feedback.
- Teams will produce a presentation and a paper to IEEE standard, creating outputs that can support their academic and professional development.
- The skills developed are transferable across study and industry, and the event also offers a chance to meet people, build networks, and have fun.

# Slide 4: Judging, Outputs, And Support

- Each team will be expected to produce a presentation and an IEEE-standard paper as part of their final submission.
- Entries will be judged by expert Electrical Engineers, ensuring that feedback and evaluation are grounded in real technical standards.
- Mentoring and templates will be provided to help teams structure their work, use AI agents effectively, and produce professional-quality outputs.

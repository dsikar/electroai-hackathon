# EEAI Hackathon Brief

- **Problem statement:** Engineering diagrams such as electrical, hydraulic, and pneumatic schematics are often locked in PDFs and images, which makes them difficult to query, analyse, validate, or convert into simulation-ready formats.
- **Challenge focus:** Students will be asked to explore how AI agents, vision-language systems, and agentic workflows can help convert engineering schematics into structured representations that support reasoning, validation, and fault analysis.
- **Why it matters:** Manual interpretation of technical diagrams is slow, error-prone, and dependent on specialist knowledge, creating clear opportunities for better engineering workflows and transferable digital skills.
- **How participants will work:** Participants will be offered workshops on AI agents and agentic processes so they can produce the work through strong, expert prompting and practical use of the tools.
- **Baseline support:** A baseline solution will be provided so teams can start quickly, understand the problem space, and build beyond an initial working approach.
- **Workshops during the week:** Participants will be able to attend workshops throughout the week to strengthen their prompting, technical approach, and project delivery.
- **Mentoring:** Students will receive expert mentoring to give them the tools and guidance needed to use AI agents effectively in producing their work.
- **Time commitment:** The expected time commitment is light and accessible: for a team of 5 members, the total commitment is 5 hours per team, based on 1 hour per member.
- **Assessment:** Entries will be judged by expert Electrical Engineers, giving students exposure to professional standards and technical feedback.
- **Outputs:** Teams will produce both a presentation and a paper written to IEEE standard, helping them build material that can support their academic and professional development.
- **Career value:** The skills developed through the hackathon, including expert prompting, agentic workflows, engineering communication, and collaborative problem-solving, are transferable across academic study and professional practice.
- **Community value:** The event gives students the chance to meet people, build networks, and have fun while working on a technically serious challenge.
- **Hackathon energy:** Participants will also have the option to take part in the coding "allnighter", adding a strong social and team-building element to the event.

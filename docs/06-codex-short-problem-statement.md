# ElectroAI Hackathon Short Problem Statement

## Short Problem Statement

Many engineering tasks still start from unstructured artefacts such as schematic images, screenshots, scans, PDFs, and photographed drawings. Engineers can read these, but software tools cannot use them directly without time-consuming manual reconstruction. At the same time, the current engineering software landscape shows uneven AI maturity: some tools provide AI in selected workflows, but image-to-structure conversion, cross-tool support, interpretation, verification, and explanation remain weak.

The ElectroAI Hackathon challenges students to build AI-agent-driven applications that can analyse engineering images and turn them into structured, design-ready, simulation-ready, or verification-ready outputs. Teams may generate intermediate representations, component and connectivity extraction, netlist scaffolds, or files that can be used in tools such as Proteus, LTspice, and KiCad. The goal is not generic OCR or a simple chatbot, but practical workflows that help engineers move from visual artefacts to usable engineering outputs.

## Ultra-Short Version

The ElectroAI Hackathon asks students to build AI-agent workflows that can take engineering images, diagrams, scans, or PDFs and turn them into structured outputs that are useful in real tools such as Proteus, LTspice, and KiCad. The challenge is justified by clear workflow gaps: image-to-structure conversion, cross-tool integration, and engineering verification remain weak across current software.

## One-Sentence Summary

Build AI-agent-driven engineering applications that turn images and diagrams into structured outputs ready for analysis, verification, simulation, or further design in tools such as Proteus, LTspice, and KiCad.

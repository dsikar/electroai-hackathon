# ElectroAI Hackathon: Short Problem Statement

---

## Short Problem Statement

Engineering artefacts — scanned schematics, exported PDFs, photographed circuits, whiteboard diagrams — are readable by engineers but not directly usable by the tools they work with. Converting a visual artefact into something a simulator or layout editor can open is still a slow, manual process. Gap analysis of the current engineering software landscape confirms that image interpretation, structured extraction, and cross-tool conversion are weak across the market, even in tools that otherwise have sophisticated AI for PCB layout or simulation optimisation.

The ElectroAI Hackathon challenges teams to build AI-agent workflows that bridge this gap: taking engineering images or diagram-like inputs, analysing them with genuine engineering understanding, and producing structured outputs ready to use in tools such as Proteus, LTspice, or KiCad. That means going beyond OCR — recognising component types, inferring connectivity, reasoning about circuit intent, and generating actionable files, not just descriptions.

Useful, partial, explainable outputs are the target. The challenge is difficult because the translation requires domain reasoning, not pattern matching alone. It is tractable because vision-language models and agentic orchestration have reached the point where meaningful engineering prototypes are within reach.

## Ultra-Short Version

Engineering diagrams are everywhere — in PDFs, scans, photos, and screenshots — but engineering tools cannot read them directly. The ElectroAI Hackathon challenges teams to build AI agents that take these visual artefacts, extract their structure and intent, and generate outputs ready to use in Proteus, LTspice, or KiCad. The problem is real, the gap is documented, and the tools to attempt it now exist.

---

## One-Sentence Summary

Build AI agents that turn engineering images and diagrams into structured, simulation-ready files — bridging the gap between what engineers can see and what engineering tools can use.

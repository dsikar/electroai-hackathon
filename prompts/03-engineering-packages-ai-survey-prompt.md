You are conducting a focused market and technology survey for the **Electrical Engineering and AI Hackathon (EEAI Hackathon)**.

Your task is to identify the **top 10 engineering software packages** relevant to:

- electrical schematic capture
- PCB layout
- electronic design automation
- systems design
- hydraulic system design
- pneumatic system design
- industrial engineering design workflows

This should include tools such as **Proteus** and **NX / Siemens**, and may include other widely used tools where relevant.

## Research Goal

We want to understand:

1. Which engineering packages are most relevant and widely used in these areas.
2. How AI is currently being used **inside** these products or **alongside** them.
3. Whether these tools include any built-in AI-assisted features for design, simulation, automation, verification, optimisation, co-pilot workflows, or documentation support.

## Key Questions To Answer

For each product, investigate:

- What the product is primarily used for
- Which engineering domain(s) it serves
- Whether it includes built-in AI features
- Whether the vendor markets any AI assistant, co-pilot, generative design, optimisation, or automation capability
- Whether AI is mainly external to the tool rather than built into it
- How AI is being used in practice with that package, if documented

## Scope

Prioritise products used in:

- schematic capture
- circuit design
- PCB design
- electrical systems design
- industrial automation design
- hydraulic design
- pneumatic design

Include a balanced mix where appropriate across:

- EDA tools
- mechanical / systems platforms
- industrial design suites
- hydraulic / pneumatic design environments

## Source Requirements

- Prefer official vendor documentation, product pages, release notes, white papers, and technical documentation.
- Use credible third-party industry sources only where needed to confirm adoption, usage, or practical AI workflows.
- Distinguish clearly between:
  - built-in AI features
  - partner or add-on AI tools
  - user-created AI workflows that are external to the package
- Do not overstate capabilities. If a tool has automation but not AI, say so clearly.

## Selection Requirement

Choose the **top 10 packages** based on a defensible combination of:

- industry relevance
- recognisability
- engineering relevance to the hackathon themes
- evidence of use in professional or educational contexts

Briefly explain why each selected package made the top 10.

## Required Output

Produce the following:

1. **Executive summary**
- A short summary of what the survey shows about the state of AI in engineering software.

2. **Top 10 package review**
- For each package:
  - product name
  - vendor
  - main engineering use case
  - relevance to electrical, PCB, hydraulic, pneumatic, or system design
  - AI built-in tools or AI-related features, if any
  - AI used alongside the product, if relevant
  - short assessment of maturity

3. **Table**
- Produce a clean table with exactly these two columns:
  - `Product Name`
  - `AI Built-In Tools`
- If no built-in AI capability is found, say `None identified`.

4. **Observations**
- Summarise patterns across the products, for example:
  - where AI is genuinely built in
  - where AI is mostly external
  - which domains appear more advanced
  - which domains still look underserved

5. **Hackathon relevance**
- Explain what this landscape suggests for the EEAI Hackathon.
- Highlight gaps where students could build useful AI-agent workflows around engineering tools.

## Output Quality Bar

The final report should be:

- concise but credible
- grounded in sources
- explicit about uncertainty
- useful for planning hackathon themes, workshops, and challenge framing

Do not produce generic AI commentary. Produce a practical survey that could be read by engineering academics, technical staff, and industry partners.
